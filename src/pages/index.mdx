## How I Work

I'm a coach and a mentor, and I do both with intention. Coaching means I ask questions and help you find your own answers. Mentoring means I look at what's in front of you and tell you what I see...then we talk about it so it makes sense.

I draw on my own experience a lot. Clients and direct reports keep telling me the "here's what I did" part has been helpful, so I share that. If you need a direct answer to make the problem less abstract, I'll give you one. If you need someone to validate your work and progress, I can do that, too.

I believe in experimentation. Not big dramatic gestures, but small moves that help you understand the problem better. Make a move, see what happens, then adjust. A lot of what we work on won't have a clean answer. That's fine. We'll try things and learn more as we go. This is how I approach my own work and my own life, and it's how I approach coaching. I can help you do the same.

</section>

## What Working With Me Looks Like

We'll have weekly hour-long sessions built around what you're actually dealing with. I keep notes across sessions so we can recognize the bigger patterns, not just whatever's on fire this week.

Sessions don't always go where we planned and that's fine. We might spend the first 15 minutes talking about life stuff or a movie we both love. We might have a detailed agenda and end up somewhere completely different, like whether you actually want to stay in tech at all. I'm here for those conversations. I'm a human being talking to other human beings, and sometimes that's what the session needs to be.
</section>

## Is Coaching a Good Fit For You?

**Yes, if you're:**
- New to tech leadership or engineering management and figuring it out as you go
- An individual contributor who wants to grow, change direction, or just figure out what you actually want from your career
- New to the industry or coming from a completely different field and could use some help navigating the new challenges of working in tech
- Someone whose brain doesn't run the way most career advice assumes works for everyone
- Looking for a real problem-solving partner who will be straight with you

**Maybe not right now, if:**
- You already know your path and things are going smoothly
- You're not ready to look at your own contribution to a hard situation. I'll always be kind about it, but I will go there
</section>
